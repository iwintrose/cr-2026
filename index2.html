03

Things to Do

Guanacaste's greatest hits
🐋
Humpback Whale Watching
August is peak humpback season in Papagayo — one of only two places on Earth where they come on both their northern and southern migrations. You are arriving at the perfect moment.
Best time: 6–8 AM departures. Calmer seas, better sightings. Book through your Star Class butler for a private boat.
🌋
Arenal Volcano Day Trip
A 3-hour drive to one of the most iconic landscapes in Central America. Hot springs at the base, zip-lining over the canopy, and volcano views at sunset that do not feel real.
Best time: Leave by 6 AM — afternoon clouds almost always roll in over Arenal in August. Mornings are clear.
🤿
Snorkelling & Scuba
Catalina Islands is the top dive spot — 45 min by boat. Sea turtles, manta rays, reef sharks. The Pacific visibility in Papagayo is exceptional, especially in the morning before wind picks up.
Best time: Before noon. The Papagayo wind kicks up every afternoon, reducing visibility and choppening conditions.
🦅
Zip-lining & Canopy Tours
Congo Trail and Diamante Eco Adventure Park are both 30–40 min from the resort. Flying through jungle canopy at speed with views of the Pacific is a full-body moment.
Best time: Midday is fine — canopy tours run in all weather. Book at least 2 days ahead in August peak season.
🏄
Surfing at Playa Tamarindo
45 minutes south — one of Costa Rica's most famous surf breaks. Green season brings consistent swells. Lessons available for all levels from multiple schools right on the beach.
Best time: 7–10 AM for lessons. Afternoon wind chop makes it trickier for beginners and the beach crowd builds fast.
🐊
Rio Tempisque Boat Safari
A slow river cruise through mangroves full of crocodiles, howler monkeys, scarlet macaws, and more bird species than most people see in a lifetime. Total immersion, zero rush.
Best time: Early morning or late afternoon. August green season means the jungle is at its most alive and lush.

Where to Eat

On-resort and nearby
⭐
Star Class Restaurant — On Resort
Exclusive AccessÀ La Carte
Star Class guests get access to the exclusive à la carte restaurant — no buffet lines, no reservations required. Butler handles your table. Steaks, fresh ceviche, tableside service every night if you want it.
💡 Ask your butler to pre-set a table by the water for your first night arrival dinner.
🦞
Ginger — Playa Hermosa
15 minPan-Asian Fusion
One of the most celebrated restaurants in Guanacaste. Pan-Asian tapas meets Costa Rican ingredients — the tuna tataki and coconut shrimp are known throughout the region. Outdoor tropical garden setting.
💡 Reservations essential. Book 3–5 days ahead. Request garden seating for the full experience.
🐟
El Ancla — Playa del Coco
20 minLocal Seafood
The local favourite for fresh-off-the-boat seafood. Order the whole fried snapper with patacones. Cash only, plastic chairs, best meal of the trip. This is the real Costa Rica — unfiltered.
💡 Go for lunch — they sell out of the best fish by 2 PM. Bring colones or USD cash.
🌅
Langosta Beach Club — Tamarindo
45 minBeach Club
Pair this with a Tamarindo surf day. Beachfront tables, excellent cocktails, wood-fired meats, and some of the best Pacific sunset views in Guanacaste. The kind of dinner you talk about for years.
💡 Arrive by 5 PM to secure a table before the sunset crowd. The cocktail hour is worth staying for.

Best Times for Everything

August intel for Guanacaste
🌅 The Pool & Beach
7–10 AM · After 4 PM
Guanacaste gets intense fast. Star Class pool is best early morning and again late afternoon when the light turns golden. August afternoons often bring brief 30-minute tropical showers — the pool clears out and the light after rain is unreal.
🐋 Whale Watching
6–10 AM Daily
Humpbacks are most active in the morning. Calmer seas mean better surface behaviour — breaching, tail slaps, the works. Afternoon swells build from the Papagayo wind and make it choppy.
🌿 Day Excursions
Leave by 6–7 AM
For Arenal, zip-lining, or Rio Tempisque — start early. Beat the heat, beat tour groups, and get back by early afternoon with the whole evening still ahead. Arriving at any jungle activity after 10 AM means fighting crowds.
🍽️ Dinner
7–9 PM
Costa Rica runs on island time. Restaurants hit their stride around 7:30 PM when the day's heat breaks. Ask your butler to book off-resort reservations for 7 PM so you get settled before the room fills up.
🌙 Nights Out
10 PM – Late
Playa del Coco and Tamarindo both have scenes that come alive after 10. The resort has its own evening entertainment — check with your butler on arrival day for the weekly calendar.
📸 Photos
Golden Hour: 5:30–6:30 PM
Pacific sunsets in August are next-level — the moisture from green season creates incredible sky colour. The resort's infinity edge and beach are perfect vantage points. Set an alarm for 5:45 PM every day.
