Curated Recommendations
Things worth buying or researching before you go — based on where you're going and what you're doing.
Sun & Water
Essential
Raw Elements Reef-Safe SPF 50
Mineral-based, certified reef-safe, and actually effective in tropical sun. Your destination legally prohibits oxybenzone and octinoxate sunscreens. This is the gold standard.
Available at MEC, Well.ca, or Amazon Canada. Get the tin — it lasts all week.
Essential
Sea to Summit Lightweight Dry Bag
For water excursions and boat tours. Your phone, wallet, and camera need protection.
8L size is perfect — big enough for phone, wallet, small camera. Under $30 at MEC.
Garmin Descent Mk2 or GoPro Hero
If you're doing any snorkel or scuba excursion, the underwater visibility is extraordinary. You'll regret not capturing it.
GoPro Hero rental also available through most dive operators on-site if you don't want to buy.
Speedo UV50+ Rash Guard
UV index at your destination averages 11–12 in {{TRIP_MONTH}}. A rash guard for snorkelling and boat tours is more practical than re-applying sunscreen every hour in the water.
Also useful for the zip-line — keeps you from getting scratched by the harness.
Clothing Worth Packing
Recommended
Vuori or Patagonia Linen Shorts
The resort has a smart-casual dress code at the {{ROOM_CLASS}} restaurant. Linen shorts with a clean shirt hits the mark without needing to pack dress pants. Pack 2–3 pairs and you're covered for every scenario.
Vuori Ripstop or Patagonia Stretch Wavefarer are both excellent. Quick-dry, look sharp.
Birkenstock Arizona Sandals
You need one sandal that can go from pool deck to dinner. The Arizona works for both. Comfortable for walking in town, presentable enough for a beach club dinner. Don't bring flip-flops as your only option.
Break them in before you leave — new Birkenstocks on day one is a classic travel mistake.
Lightweight Rain Jacket — Arc'teryx Squamish or similar
August is green season. You will get rained on at some point — a 20-minute tropical downpour, then sunshine. A packable rain jacket that fits in your daypack means you never have to cut an excursion short or get soaked on a sunset walk. This is not optional for the jungle days.
The Arc'teryx Squamish packs down to nothing. Columbia also makes excellent budget alternatives. Get something that compresses to fist-size.
Health & Wellness
Strongly Recommended
LMNT Electrolyte Packets
Your destination averages 33°C with high humidity in {{TRIP_MONTH}}. You'll be in the sun all day, likely drinking at the resort. Electrolyte packets taken every morning prevent the kind of headaches and fatigue that waste a vacation day.
Bring 10–12 packets. Citrus or watermelon flavour works well. Available at most health food stores or Amazon.
Stugeron (Cinnarizine) Motion Tablets
For boat excursions and ocean tours. Seas can be choppy in the afternoon. Stugeron is the preferred motion sickness medication for serious sailors — far more effective than Gravol.
Available OTC at most pharmacies in Canada. Take one hour before boarding. Non-drowsy formula available.
