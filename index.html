What to Do

Seven days. Make every one count.

Guanacaste in August is peak season for wildlife, warm Pacific water, and adventure. Here's what not to miss — with insider timing for each.

01
Ocean
Humpback Whale Watching
August is peak humpback season off Papagayo — one of only two places on Earth where Northern and Southern hemisphere humpbacks both come to breed. You're arriving at the exact right moment. Private boat tours available through your Star Class butler.
→ Best time: 6–8 AM departures. Calmer seas, better surface behaviour. Book 2–3 days ahead — it fills fast in August.
02
Adventure
Arenal Volcano Day Trip
Three hours south — one of the most iconic landscapes in Central America. Hot springs at the base, zip-lining over the canopy, and if the clouds clear, views of the active volcano at sunset that genuinely don't look real.
→ Leave by 6 AM. August clouds roll in over Arenal by early afternoon almost every day — mornings are your window.
03
Ocean
Snorkelling & Scuba — Catalina Islands
45 minutes by boat. Sea turtles, manta rays, reef sharks. The Pacific visibility in Papagayo is exceptional. Catalina has multiple dive sites for all levels — beginner reef snorkel to advanced wall dives.
→ Morning only. The Papagayo wind builds every afternoon — visibility drops and conditions get choppy after noon.
04
Adventure
Zip-lining & Canopy Tours
Congo Trail and Diamante Eco Adventure Park are both 30–40 min from the resort. Multiple lines, suspension bridges, rappelling. Flying through the canopy with Pacific views is a full-sensory moment.
→ Midday works — canopy tours run in all weather. Pre-book at least 2 days ahead in peak season.
05
Beach
Surfing at Playa Tamarindo
45 minutes south — one of Costa Rica's most famous surf breaks. Green season brings consistent swells. Multiple surf schools offer lessons for complete beginners. Combine it with lunch at Langosta Beach Club.
→ 7–10 AM for lessons — best conditions before afternoon wind chop builds on the water.
06
Wildlife
Rio Tempisque Boat Safari
A slow river cruise through mangroves packed with crocodiles, howler monkeys, scarlet macaws, and hundreds of bird species. Green season means the jungle is at maximum density. Zero rush. Total immersion.
→ Early morning or late afternoon — wildlife is most active at the edges of the day. August green season is the best time of year for this.
Where to Eat

On-resort and the best of Guanacaste.

Star Class ExclusiveOn Resort
Star Class À La Carte Restaurant
Planet Hollywood · Gulf of Papagayo
Star Class guests have exclusive access to the à la carte restaurant — no buffet lines, no reservations required, no limitations. Your butler handles your table. Steaks, fresh ceviche, grilled seafood, tableside service every night. This is what you paid for — use it every single night if you want.
💡 Ask your butler on arrival to pre-book a table by the water for your first night. Set the tone immediately.
15 min away
Ginger — Playa Hermosa
Playa Hermosa · Guanacaste
One of Guanacaste's most celebrated restaurants. Pan-Asian tapas meets Costa Rican ingredients — tuna tataki, coconut shrimp, and a drinks list that punches above its location. Outdoor tropical garden setting.
💡 Reserve 3–5 days ahead. Request garden seating. This is a must-do.
20 min away
El Ancla — Playa del Coco
Playa del Coco · Guanacaste
Fresh-off-the-boat seafood. Whole fried snapper with patacones. Cash only, plastic chairs, no frills. The best meal of the trip — and it'll cost less than a cocktail at the resort. The real Costa Rica.
💡 Lunch only — they sell out by 2 PM. Bring USD cash or colones.
45 min away
Langosta Beach Club — Tamarindo
Tamarindo · Guanacaste
Combine with the Tamarindo surf day. Beachfront tables, wood-fired meats, killer cocktails, and Pacific sunset views that justify the drive alone. The kind of dinner you still talk about five years later.
💡 Arrive by 5 PM for a table before sunset. The golden hour here is extraordinary.
August Intel

Right place. Right time.

Pool & Beach
7–10 AM
4–6 PM
Guanacaste heat builds fast. The Star Class pool is best early morning and again late afternoon in golden light. August afternoons bring brief tropical showers — 20–30 minutes, then it's clear again. The light after rain is spectacular for photos.
Whale Watching
6–10 AM
Humpbacks are most active in the morning. Calm seas mean better surface behaviour — breaching, fin slaps, tail dives. Afternoon Papagayo wind builds swells that make for a rougher ride and fewer sightings.
Day Excursions
Depart by 6–7 AM
For Arenal, zip-lining, or any jungle activity — start before the heat builds and before tour groups arrive. You'll be back at the resort by early afternoon with the whole evening still ahead. Arriving late means crowds and cloud cover.
Dining Out
7–9 PM
Costa Rica runs on island time. Restaurants hit their stride at 7:30 PM when the day cools down. Ask your butler to time off-resort reservations for 7 PM — you'll get settled before the room fills and catch the last of the evening light.
Sunset Photos
5:45–6:30 PM
Pacific sunsets in August are exceptional. The green season moisture creates deep orange and purple sky colour. The resort's infinity edge and beach are perfect vantage points. Set an alarm for 5:45 PM every single day — don't miss a single one.
Nights Out
10 PM – Late
Playa del Coco and Tamarindo have bar scenes that properly come alive after 10. The resort itself has evening entertainment — ask your butler on arrival for the weekly schedule and book ahead for any shows or events.
